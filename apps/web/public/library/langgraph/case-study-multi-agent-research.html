Case Study 01 · Advanced · 25 minutes

Design a multi-agent research system.

  • Problem: produce interconnected market analysis, not five separate summaries.
  • Challenge: coordinate specialists without uncontrolled communication or bloated state.
  • Goal: make every architectural decision explainable, observable, and testable.
5 specialist agentsCollaborative stateAdaptive workflow5 review questions
1 Frame the problem

What must the system accomplish?

Business need

  • Consulting clients need comprehensive market reports.
  • Claims need evidence, context, and implications.
  • Findings must connect across research domains.
  • The final report must expose uncertainty.

System design pressure

  • Specialists depend on one another's findings.
  • Some work can run in parallel; some cannot.
  • New evidence can trigger more investigation.
  • Synthesis must wait for evidence maturity.
Anti-goal
  • Do not run five agents independently and concatenate their answers.
  • That creates multiple outputs, not collaborative intelligence.

Design checkpoint: what are we optimizing for?

Quality
  • Connected reasoning
  • Source traceability
Control
  • Clear ownership
  • Bounded handoffs
Adaptability
  • Question-specific teams
  • Backtracking
2 Choose the architecture

Should every agent talk to every other agent?

  • Direct communication looks flexible at small scale.
  • Five agents can create 20 directed relationships.
  • A coordinator reduces the control surface to explicit worker handoffs.

Compare the two designs

Decision
  • Use a coordinator for control and observability.
  • Allow collaboration through structured state and explicit events.
  • Do not make the coordinator perform specialist analysis.
