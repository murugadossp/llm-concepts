This chapter demystifies how a Large Language Model (<abbr title="Large Language Model">LLM</abbr>) evolves from a random matrix of numbers into a helpful, conversational assistant.

<Callout title="Learning goal">
  By the end of this module, you should be able to explain the difference between:
  * **Pre-training**: Next-token prediction at scale to learn grammar and facts.
  * **Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)**: Teaching conversation structure and formatting.
  * **Alignment & RLHF**: Nudging outputs toward human preferences and safety values.
  * **Distillation**: Compressing large models into faster student models.
</Callout>

## The Three-Stage Roadmap

To go from a raw mathematical formula to the assistant in your chat bar, models pass through three distinct phases:

1. **Pre-training**: Next-token prediction at planetary scale. The model learns grammar, syntax, facts, and reasoning shortcuts by reading trillions of tokens. This stage is extremely expensive, costing millions of dollars in compute power.
2. **Supervised Fine-Tuning (<abbr title="Supervised Fine-Tuning">SFT</abbr>)**: Feeding the model high-quality demonstration data of prompts and ideal responses. This forces the model to transition from a generic text completion engine into a conversational assistant.
3. **Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (<abbr title="Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback">RLHF</abbr>)**: Tuning the final responses by letting humans rank candidate answers. This teaches the model to align with safe, helpful guidelines.

<RememberCard>
  - Pre-training builds the base representation (world facts, reasoning).
  - Fine-tuning formats that representation into a structured chatbot.
  - RLHF aligns the tone, safety, and helpfulness boundaries.
</RememberCard>
